Coordinates, what are they?

If you have ever used mspaint or other drawing program, you know that you can see two numbers changing as you move the mouse cursor. Usually the first number is the x coordinate and the second is the y coordinate.

These two numbers together uniquely identify the position of the mouse cursor on the screen. The numbers themselves express a length against a dimension.

In real life there are three dimensions, the x, y and z dimensions, but since a computer monitor or a paper page is two-dimensional and flat, we only need two of them.

The coordinates were invented by this guy called Descartes, aka Big D in 1637. For this achievement, the field of cartography was named after him, since it heavily uses coordinates.

Note

In addition to the dimensions of x, y, and z there is also a t or time dimension. If you align your chakras right and open your third eye, you can freely drop it from equations, as it becomes irrelevant.

Usually you depict the dimensions as little arrows on paper. These arrows are the axii. You can find the coordinate of a point by drawing a line perpendicular to an axis from a point to that axis.


fig 1.2.1. Depiction of coordinates and their axii.

A coordinate can represent a point, a vector or basically any other thing that you want. The possibilities are literally endless.
