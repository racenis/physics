Measurements, what are they?

There are only 3 different units in mechanics:

Meter – Unit of Length
It is equal to 1 650 763,73 wave lengths of the krypton isotope Kr86 as it emits light in a vacuum, its atoms transitioning from state 5d to state 2p.
1 meter is approximately 1/40 000 000th of an Earth's meridian's length. It is divided into 100 centimeters and 1000 millimeters, 1000 meters are a single kilometer.
Kilogram – Unit of Mass
It is the mass of a little widget kept in the French National archive. The mass of this little widget is approximately equal to 1 dm3 water weight at 4Â°C temperature. 1000 kilograms are a single ton and 1000 grams are a kilogram.
Second – Unit of Time
It is the duration that the cesium isotope Cs133 atoms take to emit radiation for 9 192 631 770 periods, in their transition between the two hyperfine levels of the ground state.

This is the “SI” system. Every other possible measurement can be expressed in these three units.

Some people think that there are such units as “inches” and “ounces”. These, in fact, are totally fake made up units. Using them is akin to believing in astrology or economics. Anyone who purports to use them is a lunatic. Disregard anything they say.

Fun Fact ... or is it (fun)?

If someone in France decided to shave off some bits off of the kilogram, all of the scales in the world would suddenly be incorrect. It's almost as if there is some kind of a conspiracy.

Force is the thing you do when you push a thing or squeeze a thing. It is measured in newtons (N). They can be exerted directly (like punching someone in the face) or by a distance (like hurting someones feelings).

A single newton is the force necessary to accelerate an object with a mass of 1 kg by 1 m/s2 speed.

The unit of pressure is a pascal (Pa) and it is equal to one newton per meter squared (N/m2).

Note

The pascal measurement unit is different from Pascal, the programming language, and Pascal, the guy who invented said programming language. This distinction will be crucial later, when we'll be writing simulation programs in Pascal.

If you haven't noticed by now, then measurements are basically numbers with a couple letters attached to them. Physicians like to pretend otherwise.

Usually you don't write the letters in equations, though. You just write the number part.
